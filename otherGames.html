Games that served as inspirations

While I would like this game to feel like an original creation, like most games, it stems from inspirations found in other games
There are a couple of main games that supplied inspiration for me and they are as follows.

  • Asteroids
    • Published in 1979 by Atari | Developed by Atari
    • A simple Shoot 'em up game based in outer space where the player controls a spaceship and tries to survive an
      endless amount of asteroids to earn points and get a high score.
  • Rogue
    • Published in 1980 by Epyx | Developed by A.I. Design
    • A procedurally generated turn-based dungeon crawler that uses an ASCII art style.
      It is well known for the fact that when you die, you restart from the beginning with nothing and the fact that every time you play the game is slightly different.
  • Nova Drift
    • Published in 2024 by Pixeljam | Developed by Chimeric
    • A Roguelike game based on Asteroids that, while it has the same inspirations that I did, it took a different path.
      Despite this, it still serves as a source of inspiration and I had already planned to have a different style of upgrading
  • Touhou Project
    • Published in 1997 by Team Shangai Alice | Developed by Team Shangai Alice and Twilight Frontier
    • A bullet hell shoot 'em up game where you have to dodge a large amount of projectiles while
      destroying enemies to gain points.
    • This game served as less of an inspiration than the others, but it still contributed to my love of games in the
      shoot 'em up and bullet hell genres.
